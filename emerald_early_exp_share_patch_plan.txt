POKEMON EMERALD EARLY EXP SHARE PATCH PLAN

Goal
Reduce early-game grinding by making Exp. Share available immediately and changing it into a modern-style party-wide progression tool.

BEST VERSION RECOMMENDATION

Implement Exp. Share as an early-game convenience feature with the following design:

1. Add Exp. Share to the first Poké Mart
- Exp. Share should be sold in the first Poké Mart inventory available to the player.
- Price should be set to 0.
- The item should be obtainable immediately without requiring grinding or progression gates.

2. Rework Exp. Share into a modern party-wide Exp. Share
- Exp. Share should no longer function only as a held Gen 3 split-EXP item.
- Instead, it should behave like a modern Exp. Share effect:
  - after battle, the full party receives experience
  - the player does not need to equip the item to a single Pokémon
  - the item functions as an account-wide or bag-based progression unlock once obtained

3. Preserve clean progression
- The patch should reduce grind without making the game feel completely automatic.
- The system should help players train full teams, rotate party members, and experiment with more Pokémon.
- The change should fit the broader rebalance philosophy of reducing the need for cheating while supporting catching, team building, and experimentation.

PRICE DECISION

Exp. Share price:
- 0

Reason:
- Exp. Share is being used here as a quality-of-life progression tool, not a luxury purchase.
- Pricing it at 0 guarantees immediate access and removes early-game friction.
- This is especially appropriate in a rebalance that is already trying to reduce grind and improve pacing.

DESIGN INTENT

This patch is meant to solve one of the biggest early-game problems in older Pokémon games:
- training a full party takes too long
- boxed or lower-level Pokémon fall behind quickly
- players feel pushed into overusing one or two party members
- lack of early Exp. Share increases grind pressure

By putting Exp. Share in the first Poké Mart for free and making it party-wide, the player gains:
- smoother early-game leveling
- better team variety
- less grinding
- easier experimentation with catches
- more natural progression without cheats

IMPLEMENTATION PHILOSOPHY

This should be treated as a gameplay system update, not just an item placement edit.

Required outcome:
- the first Poké Mart sells Exp. Share for 0
- once obtained, Exp. Share provides party-wide EXP behavior

Preferred behavior model:
- Exp. Share works from the bag or as a passive unlock once acquired
- the player does not need to assign it as a held item
- the entire team benefits after battle in a modern-style format

BALANCE NOTE

Because this patch is intended to work alongside the existing progression rebalance, it should be reviewed together with:
- increased wild EXP
- increased trainer EXP
- cheaper shop prices
- wild battle money rewards

If leveling becomes too fast in actual testing, the preferred response is to tune global EXP numbers slightly rather than removing early Exp. Share access.

FINAL IMPLEMENTATION TARGET

Use this exact target:
- Exp. Share added to the first Poké Mart
- Exp. Share price set to 0
- Exp. Share behavior changed to modern party-wide EXP sharing
- System implemented as a clean early-game quality-of-life feature

SUMMARY

The best version of this patch is not just selling the vanilla Exp. Share early.
The best version is:
- sell Exp. Share in the first Poké Mart
- set its price to 0
- convert it into a modern party-wide EXP Share effect

That gives the player immediate access to one of the most important anti-grind tools in the game and fits the overall rebalance direction much better than leaving it as a normal held item.
