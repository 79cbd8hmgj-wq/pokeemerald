POKEMON EMERALD MOVE REWORK SPEC V2
===================================

Purpose
-------
This file turns the move audit into an implementation-ready first-pass spec.
It focuses on weak, low-usage, low-reward, and poorly supported moves from the
Generation 3 move pool and gives each one a clearer gameplay role.

Design Rules
------------
1. Every attacking move should serve a purpose:
   - reliable STAB
   - utility damage
   - setup payoff
   - early-game bridge move
   - high-risk burst
   - archetype support
2. Weak move types should get consistency before raw damage spikes.
3. Niche status moves should either become broadly usable or remain niche on purpose.
4. Avoid power creep on already-good signature and top-tier moves.
5. Preserve Pokemon identity wherever possible; use retyping sparingly.

Source Basis
------------
This spec is based on the uploaded move catalog covering the 103 Generation 3
moves introduced across Ruby, Sapphire, FireRed, LeafGreen, and Emerald.

SECTION 2 - EXACT BUFF SPECS
------------------------------------------------------------------

1. ASTONISH
Current: Ghost / Physical / 30 BP / 100 Acc / 15 PP / may flinch
New:     Ghost / Physical / 50 BP / 100 Acc / 20 PP / 20% flinch chance
Goal: Turn Astonish into a usable early-game Ghost STAB without replacing
mid-game Ghost attacks.
Why: Ghost has very shallow move support in this Gen 3 move set, and 30 BP is
far too weak to matter.

2. ARM THRUST
Current: Fighting / Physical / 15 BP / 100 Acc / 20 PP / hits 2-5 times
New:     Fighting / Physical / 20 BP / 100 Acc / 20 PP / hits 2-5 times
Goal: Give Fighting multi-hit users a move that does meaningful chip and breaks
substitutes or sturdy-style gameplay more reliably.
Why: 15 BP leaves the move too weak on average.

3. BULLET SEED
Current: Grass / Physical / 25 BP / 100 Acc / 30 PP / hits 2-5 times
New:     Grass / Physical / 20 BP / 100 Acc / 30 PP / hits 3-5 times
Goal: Make it more consistent rather than just stronger on maximum rolls.
Why: Multi-hit Grass should support physical Grass users, but 2-hit low rolls
feel too bad.
Implementation note: If guaranteed 3-5 hits is awkward in-engine, fallback to
25 BP with improved distribution only.

4. GRASS WHISTLE
Current: Grass / Status / -- / 55 Acc / 15 PP / sleep
New:     Grass / Status / -- / 70 Acc / 15 PP / sleep
Goal: Keep it inaccurate enough to feel risky but not unusable.
Why: 55 accuracy pushes it below practical use outside gimmicks.

5. NEEDLE ARM
Current: Grass / Physical / 60 BP / 100 Acc / 15 PP / may flinch
New:     Grass / Physical / 70 BP / 100 Acc / 15 PP / 20% flinch chance
Goal: Create a respectable mid-tier physical Grass move for aggressive users.
Why: It currently feels too close to filler.

6. MUD SHOT
Current: Ground / Special / 55 BP / 95 Acc / 15 PP / lowers Speed
New:     Ground / Special / 65 BP / 95 Acc / 15 PP / lowers Speed by 1 stage
Goal: Give special Ground users a legitimate utility STAB.
Why: Ground is under-supported and Mud Shot is too weak as a bridge move.

7. SAND TOMB
Current: Ground / Physical / 35 BP / 85 Acc / 15 PP / traps 4-5 turns
New:     Ground / Physical / 50 BP / 90 Acc / 15 PP / traps 4-5 turns
Goal: Make trapping sets actually threaten damage.
Why: The original version is too inaccurate and too weak at once.

8. AIR CUTTER
Current: Flying / Special / 60 BP / 95 Acc / 25 PP / high crit ratio
New:     Flying / Special / 75 BP / 95 Acc / 20 PP / high crit ratio
Goal: Give special Flying attackers a real mid-tier attack.
Why: Special Flying is unusually shallow and Air Cutter is underpowered.

9. ICE BALL
Current: Ice / Physical / 30 BP / 90 Acc / 20 PP / doubles for 5 turns
New:     Ice / Physical / 40 BP / 90 Acc / 15 PP / doubles for 5 turns
Goal: Make rollout-style Ice use more threatening without making it universal.
Why: It is too weak to justify its lock-in drawback.

10. ICICLE SPEAR
Current: Ice / Physical / 25 BP / 100 Acc / 30 PP / hits 2-5 times
New:      Ice / Physical / 20 BP / 100 Acc / 30 PP / hits 3-5 times
Goal: Same philosophy as Bullet Seed: consistency over random disappointment.
Why: Ice needs better physical tools, but not reckless raw power.
Implementation note: Same fallback as Bullet Seed if hit-range edits are hard.

11. POISON FANG
Current: Poison / Physical / 50 BP / 100 Acc / 15 PP / may badly poison
New:     Poison / Physical / 65 BP / 100 Acc / 15 PP / 30% badly poison chance
Goal: Make this the reliable offensive Poison pressure move.
Why: Poison is starved for attacks and 50 BP is not enough.

12. POISON TAIL
Current: Poison / Physical / 50 BP / 100 Acc / 25 PP / high crit, may poison
New:     Poison / Physical / 60 BP / 100 Acc / 20 PP / high crit, 20% poison chance
Goal: Make Poison Tail the more offense-oriented counterpart to Poison Fang.
Why: It needs a clearer role split from Poison Fang.

13. ROCK BLAST
Current: Rock / Physical / 25 BP / 90 Acc / 10 PP / hits 2-5 times
New:     Rock / Physical / 20 BP / 90 Acc / 10 PP / hits 3-5 times
Goal: Give Rock a more dependable multi-hit option.
Why: Rock is under-supported and low-roll Rock Blast feels terrible.
Implementation note: Same fallback model as Bullet Seed if hit scripting is limited.

14. MAGICAL LEAF
Current: Grass / Special / 60 BP / never misses / 20 PP
New:     Grass / Special / 70 BP / never misses / 20 PP
Goal: Give special Grass users a smoother reliable option.
Why: It should stand out as a consistency move, not just weak filler.

15. WATER PULSE
Current: Water / Special / 60 BP / 100 Acc / 20 PP / may confuse
New:     Water / Special / 70 BP / 100 Acc / 20 PP / 20% confusion chance
Goal: Improve early-to-mid Water special offense.
Why: Too many Water users lean on Surf later; this helps bridge progression.

SECTION 3 - EXACT REPURPOSE SPECS
------------------------------------------------------------------

These moves should not just be numerically buffed. Their actual role is too
narrow, too awkward, or too low-value for most players.

1. ASSIST
Current role: random ally move
New concept: User calls a random move from the party, but cannot call two-turn,
OHKO, self-KO, or field-only moves.
Optional bonus: +1 priority
Goal: Keep the fun chaos identity while removing dead-roll frustration.
Why: Randomness is fine; useless randomness is not.

2. CAMOUFLAGE
Current role: changes type based on location
New: Normal / Status / 20 PP
Effect: Changes the user to a terrain-linked defensive type and grants +1 Sp. Def.
Goal: Turn it into a flexible defensive adaptation tool.
Why: Pure type change with inconsistent map logic is too awkward for regular play.

3. FLATTER
Current role: confuses target and raises target's Sp. Atk
New: Dark / Status / 15 PP / 100 Acc
Effect: Confuses target and lowers target's Sp. Def by 2 stages.
Goal: Create an offensive disruption/setup move for Dark and special attackers.
Why: Raising the foe's Sp. Atk is usually more harmful than helpful.

4. GRUDGE
Current role: if user faints, foe's last move loses all PP
New: Ghost / Status / 10 PP
Effect: If the user faints before its next turn, the attacker loses 5 PP from the
move used and suffers -1 Attack and -1 Sp. Atk.
Goal: Preserve sacrificial ghost-tech identity but make it broadly useful.
Why: Full-PP drain is binary and often irrelevant in regular play.

5. IMPRISON
Current role: foe cannot use moves the user knows
New: Psychic / Status / 10 PP
Effect: For 5 turns, opposing Pokemon cannot use healing, protection, or stat-boosting moves.
Goal: Create an anti-setup, anti-stall control move.
Why: The original effect is too matchup-specific.

6. MUD SPORT
Current role: weakens Electric-type moves
New: Ground / Status / 15 PP
Effect: For 5 turns, grounded Pokemon recover 1/16 max HP each turn and Electric moves lose 25% power.
Goal: Turn it into a meaningful Ground-control field move.
Why: The original effect is too narrow to justify a moveslot.

7. SNATCH
Current role: steals next status move effect
New: Dark / Status / 10 PP / +1 priority
Effect: Steals the opponent's next healing or stat-boosting move this turn. If none is used, user gains +1 Speed.
Goal: Make prediction-based Dark utility less all-or-nothing.
Why: Pure miss-or-win design is too inconsistent.

8. TORMENT
Current role: target cannot use same move twice in a row
New: Dark / Status / 15 PP / 100 Acc
Effect: Target cannot use the same move twice in a row for 4 turns and loses 1 PP from its last used move immediately.
Goal: Improve anti-spam disruption.
Why: Existing Torment is interesting but too low-pressure.

9. MUD SPORT / WATER SPORT PAIRING PRINCIPLE
These two should be parallel field-control moves rather than nearly dead buttons.

10. WATER SPORT
Current role: weakens Fire-type moves
New: Water / Status / 15 PP
Effect: For 5 turns, all Pokemon heal 1/16 HP at end of turn if rain is active;
Fire moves lose 25% power regardless, and Water moves gain 10% accuracy.
Goal: Create a broad water-support field move.
Why: Original effect is far too niche.

11. SPIT UP
Current role: spends Stockpile to attack
New: Normal / Special / variable / 100 Acc / 10 PP
Effect: Base power now scales more aggressively with Stockpile and does not remove all defensive bonuses from Stockpile until after damage resolves.
Goal: Make Stockpile sets worth considering.
Why: The stockpile package is too low-payoff unless heavily supported.

SECTION 4 - RETYPE CANDIDATE
------------------------------------------------------------------

Use retyping carefully. Only one move is recommended for the first wave.

1. SMELLING SALTS
Current: Normal / Physical / 70 BP / doubles on paralyzed targets, then cures paralysis
Recommended New Type: Fighting
Keep effect: Power doubles if target is paralyzed, then cures paralysis
Goal: Give Fighting an offbeat utility move and help certain under-supported
Fighting or physical support archetypes.
Why: As Normal, it lacks strong identity and often feels random.
Risk: Could become unusually strong on specific users if distribution is too wide.
Control this through learnsets.

Fallback option:
- Keep it Normal but raise BP to 80.
- Retype only if you want broader Fighting move variety.

------------------------------------------------------------------

Types benefiting most from this spec:

Poison
- Poison Fang buff
- Poison Tail buff
Result: Poison gets usable physical pressure.

Rock
- Rock Blast consistency buff
Result: Rock gets better chip and anti-sub tools.

Ghost
- Astonish buff
- Grudge repurpose
Result: Ghost gets an actual early attack plus a better utility trap.

Ground
- Mud Shot buff
- Sand Tomb buff
- Mud Sport repurpose
Result: Ground becomes much more than just Earthquake later.

Flying
- Air Cutter buff
Result: Special Flying users gain a needed bridge move.

Grass
- Bullet Seed consistency buff
- Grass Whistle accuracy buff
- Needle Arm buff
- Magical Leaf buff
Result: Grass gains better early and mid-game options on both sides.

Ice
- Ice Ball buff
- Icicle Spear consistency buff
Result: Ice gets better physical move support.

Water
- Water Pulse buff
- Water Sport repurpose
Result: Water support and progression improve.

Dark
- Flatter repurpose
- Snatch repurpose
- Torment repurpose
Result: Dark becomes more disruptive and interactive.

------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 8 - LEARNSET WARNING
------------------------------------------------------------------

Do not distribute improved moves blindly.

Most dangerous overbuff risks come from:
- Poison types gaining both stronger STAB and improved status spread at once
- Fast users receiving stronger never-miss or speed-control moves
- Bulky utility Pokemon receiving too many improved disruption tools together
- Fighting users abusing a retyped Smelling Salts too efficiently

Recommended rule:
- First implement move data.
- Then review distribution one family at a time.
