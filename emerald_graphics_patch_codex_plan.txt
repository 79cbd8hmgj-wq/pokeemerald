POKÉMON EMERALD MOD – FULL GRAPHICS PATCH IMPLEMENTATION PLAN FOR CODEX

Purpose
This document is a full graphics patch plan for a Pokémon Emerald decomp project. It is written so Codex can use it as an implementation brief instead of guessing what “improve the graphics” means.

Core Goal
Raise the visual quality of the game as much as realistically possible while still keeping the project stable, readable, and clearly GBA-native.

Important Constraint
Do not attempt to increase the internal game resolution. The Game Boy Advance display is 240x160 and Emerald is built around tile, sprite, and palette workflows. “Better graphics” here means better art direction, better asset quality, better palette work, cleaner UI, stronger battle presentation, and more consistent visual polish.

Primary Design Standard
Target a “premium late-era GBA” presentation.
The game should still feel like Pokémon Emerald, but noticeably cleaner, richer, and more polished in every major visual area.

Do Not Do These
- Do not convert the project into a fake HD or higher-resolution game.
- Do not mix wildly different art styles.
- Do not insert oversized sprites that clash with the engine.
- Do not prioritize experimental rendering rewrites over visible asset improvements.
- Do not make one-off edits with no style guide.
- Do not break existing graphics pipelines unless replacement pipelines are fully documented.

Preferred Visual Direction
- Keep the game GBA-native in proportion and readability.
- Use richer palettes, sharper contrast, and cleaner tile art.
- Upgrade battle scenes heavily.
- Upgrade key overworld sprites and tilesets.
- Improve UI clarity and consistency.
- Make the game feel more vivid, less flat, and less washed out.

PROJECT SCOPE
This graphics patch should be treated as a major visual overhaul with these five pillars:

1. Battle Presentation Overhaul
2. Pokémon and Trainer Sprite Upgrade
3. Overworld Sprite Upgrade
4. Tileset and Map Palette Overhaul
5. UI and Visual Feedback Polish

First audit the current graphics structure of the repo before changing assets.

Objectives
- Identify all current graphics directories and file rules.
- Identify how battle backgrounds, Pokémon sprites, trainer sprites, UI assets, overworld sprites, and tilesets are currently stored and built.
- Identify whether the repo is closer to pret pokeemerald or pokeemerald-expansion conventions.
- Identify whether any custom graphics rules already exist.
- Produce a short internal summary of where each major visual asset category lives.

Expected Output
A short audit summary in comments or a markdown file that lists:
- battle backgrounds locations
- Pokémon battle sprites locations
- trainer sprite locations
- overworld sprite locations
- tileset locations

- palette locations

Implementation Rule
Do not begin bulk replacements until the asset pipeline is understood.

==================================================
PHASE 2 – BATTLE BACKGROUND OVERHAUL
==================================================

Priority
Very high. This is one of the biggest visible upgrades in the entire patch.

Goal
Replace flat or dated battle backgrounds with richer, cleaner, more visually distinct backgrounds while remaining readable on GBA hardware.

Targets
- grass battle background
- cave battle background
- water battle background
- forest battle background
- mountain/rock battle background
- indoor battle background
- urban/town battle background if applicable
- special encounter backgrounds where practical
- elite four/champion/gym leader/villain major battle backgrounds if the repo supports unique scenes

Art Direction Rules
- Use stronger depth and horizon separation.
- Improve environmental detail without making the background noisy.
- Preserve battle readability above all else.
- Keep the battler area visually clear so sprites do not get lost.
- Use palette ramps that are vibrant but not neon-saturated.
- Avoid muddy low-contrast colors.

Technical Rules
- Keep asset sizes and formats consistent with the engine’s battle background system.
- Respect palette limitations.
- Preserve alignment and layer behavior.
- Verify that no background breaks animations, battle transitions, or status display readability.

Codex Actions
- Audit existing battle background asset references.
- Replace backgrounds category by category.
- Normalize palettes where possible.
- Test each major battle environment in-game.
- Ensure text boxes, HP bars, move names, and battler sprites remain clear on every background.

Success Criteria
Battle scenes immediately look more modern, more location-specific, and less flat.

==================================================
PHASE 3 – POKÉMON BATTLE SPRITE UPGRADE
==================================================

Priority
Very high.

Goal
Improve front sprites, back sprites, party icons if applicable, and any related species visual assets so battles feel sharper and more premium.

Targets
- player-side back sprites
- enemy front sprites
- shiny sprite consistency where applicable
- species icons/menu icons if supported by the repo
- sprite alignment and shadow compatibility

Art Direction Rules
- Keep sprite proportions compatible with Gen 3 battle framing.
- Prefer polished Gen 3+ or Gen 4-inspired sprite quality, but keep consistency across the full Pokédex.
- Avoid mixing clean modern sprites with low-detail legacy sprites.
- Preserve silhouette readability.
- Avoid over-detailing tiny sprites.
- Keep outlines and contrast readable on hardware-appropriate palettes.

Technical Rules
- Do not let sprites overflow or clip incorrectly in battle.
- Maintain proper anchor points.
- Preserve animation compatibility where animations depend on sprite dimensions or positioning.
- Ensure shiny variants and palette swaps still work correctly.

Codex Actions
- Audit species sprite files and insertion rules.
- Upgrade sprites in structured batches.
- Verify battle placement and centering.
- Verify that no species has broken sprite references.
- Verify female/alternate forms if the repo supports them.

Success Criteria
Pokémon battles look cleaner and more visually impressive without looking out of place in Emerald.

PHASE 6 – TILESSET OVERHAUL AND MAP PALETTE PASS
==================================================

Priority
Very high.

Goal
Improve the look of the world itself through stronger tiles, cleaner edges, better texture work, and much better palette discipline.

Highest Priority Tileset Categories
1. Outdoor nature routes
2. Town exterior tiles
3. Water and shoreline tiles
4. Forest tiles
5. Cave tiles
6. Interior house/shop/gym tiles

Art Direction Rules
- Increase visual richness without destroying readability.
- Improve grass, dirt, cliffs, tree lines, and water transitions.
- Reduce flatness and repeated-looking surfaces where practical.
- Use better contrast to make maps feel less washed out.
- Keep collision readability intact.
- Keep doors, ledges, stairs, and interactable tiles obvious.

Palette Rules
- Outdoor greens should be cleaner and more deliberate.
- Water should look more vibrant and less dull.
- Interior palettes should be readable and less muddy.
- Town palettes should support clearer visual identity between locations.
- Avoid oversaturation that causes eye strain.

Codex Actions
- Audit current primary and secondary tilesets.
- Build a prioritized replacement list.
- Improve palettes first where full redraws are too large.
- Improve core outdoor tiles next.
- Improve town and interior tiles after that.
- Verify all edited tiles in Porymap-compatible workflows if relevant.
- Check tile behavior and metatile references after edits.

Success Criteria
Routes, towns, caves, and interiors all look cleaner, richer, and more intentionally art-directed.
