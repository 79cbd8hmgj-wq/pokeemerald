POKEMON EMERALD ECONOMY + PROGRESSION REBALANCE PLAN

Goal
Reduce the need to cheat for XP and money by making normal gameplay more rewarding, less grind-heavy, and more supportive of team building, catching, and experimentation.

DIRECT RECOMMENDATION

1. EXP CHANGES
- Wild battle EXP multiplier: x1.6
- Trainer battle EXP multiplier: x1.3

Design intent:
- Wild battles should feel worth doing instead of feeling like a waste of time.
- Trainer battles should still be important, but wild grinding should no longer be miserable.
- This keeps progression smoother without making the game completely trivial.

2. MART / ITEM PRICE CHANGES
- All marts prices: x0.6 of vanilla cost

Design intent:
- Essential items should be affordable enough to actually use.
- Players should not feel punished for buying healing items, Poké Balls, status heals, or utility items.
- Lower prices support roster experimentation, catching, and breeding.

Examples of what this means:
- A 300 cost item becomes 180
- A 500 cost item becomes 300
- A 1000 cost item becomes 600
- A 2000 cost item becomes 1200

3. MONEY REWARD CHANGES
- Wild battles now award money
- Wild money payout should scale low, at roughly 1/4 of trainer equivalent payout

Design intent:
- Every battle should contribute something
- Trainers should remain the best source of money
- Wild battles should provide enough money to feel useful, but not so much that the economy breaks

Recommended payout philosophy:
- Common low-level unevolved wild Pokémon should give small payouts
- Stronger, rarer, higher-level, or evolved wild Pokémon should give somewhat better payouts
- Trainer payouts should still remain clearly superior overall

WHY THIS PACKAGE WORKS

1. Fixes the need to cheat
If XP comes faster, prices are lower, and all battles give money, the player no longer feels forced to use cheats just to keep up.

2. Supports team variety
A healthier economy makes it easier to:
- raise more than one team
- catch more Pokémon
- test different movesets
- use breeding systems more often
- recover from bad purchases or bad training decisions

3. Preserves progression
This package is strong, but not extreme.
- EXP is boosted, but not enough to completely destroy pacing
- Prices are lowered, but not to the point where everything is free
- Wild money exists, but trainers still matter more

BALANCE PHILOSOPHY

The economy should support:
- team building
- experimentation
- catching
- training
- breeding

The economy should not:
- force hoarding
- punish item use
- make grinding feel worthless
- push the player toward cheating for basic progression

IMPLEMENTATION PRIORITY

Recommended order of testing:
1. Apply EXP multipliers
2. Apply mart price reduction
3. Add wild battle money rewards
4. Playtest progression from early game through midgame

PLAYTEST CHECKPOINTS

Focus on these questions during testing:
- Does early game still feel paced correctly?
- Does the player level too quickly before the first few gyms?
- Can the player afford enough balls and healing items without feeling rich too early?
- Do wild battles feel worthwhile for both EXP and money?
- Do trainer battles still feel more valuable than wild encounters?
- Does the player feel free to experiment with more Pokémon without breaking the economy?

FINAL IMPLEMENTATION TARGET

Use this as the first-pass balance package:
- Wild EXP: x1.6
- Trainer EXP: x1.3
- Mart prices: x0.6
- Wild battles award money
- Wild money payout: about 25% of trainer-equivalent scaling

SUMMARY

This rebalance is meant to make Pokémon Emerald feel less restrictive and less grind-dependent without removing progression tension completely. The player should be rewarded for battling, catching, and building a team through normal gameplay rather than needing cheats to make the experience feel fair.
