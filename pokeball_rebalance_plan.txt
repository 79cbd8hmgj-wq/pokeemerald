POKE BALL REBALANCE PLAN - POKEMON EMERALD MOD
===============================================

CORE DESIGN GOAL
----------------
Make Poke Balls matter more so catching decisions depend on timing, preparation,
encounter type, and utility instead of just buying Ultra Balls later and ignoring
everything else.

FINAL ACTIVE BALL SET
---------------------
Core progression:
- Poke Ball
- Great Ball
- Ultra Ball
- Master Ball

Specialist balls:
- Quick Ball
- Timer Ball
- Repeat Ball
- Level Ball
- Lure Ball
- Heal Ball

Dropped from the planned system:
- Net Ball
- Snare Ball / repurposed Luxury Ball concept
- Heavy Ball
- Cherish Ball
- Dive Ball
- Nest Ball

FINAL IDENTITIES AND EFFECTS
----------------------------

1) Poke Ball
- Role: Baseline capture item
- Effect: 1.0x catch rate

2) Great Ball
- Role: Standard early upgrade
- Effect: 1.5x catch rate

3) Ultra Ball
- Role: Premium default ball
- Effect: 2.0x catch rate

4) Master Ball
- Role: Ultimate rare catch item
- Effect: Guaranteed catch

5) Quick Ball
- Role: Fast catch specialist
- Effect: 4.0x catch rate on turn 1, 1.0x after
- Purpose: Rewards immediate decision-making and gives players a reason to carry
  a specialized ball even early in the game

6) Timer Ball
- Role: Long-battle specialist
- Effect: Starts at 1.0x and scales upward each turn, capping at 4.0x
- Suggested implementation: Increase gradually every turn and hit the cap around
  turn 10 to 12
- Purpose: Rewards long, controlled capture attempts and gives a clear contrast
  with Quick Ball

7) Repeat Ball
- Role: Collection / farming specialist
- Effect: 3.5x if the species has already been caught before, 1.0x otherwise
- Purpose: Makes dex cleanup, ability hunting, and repeat catches meaningfully
  easier

8) Level Ball
- Role: Strength advantage specialist
- Effect options:
  Preferred mid-strength version:
  - 2.0x if your Pokemon's level is higher than the target's
  - 3.5x if your Pokemon's level is at least double the target's
  - 5.0x if your Pokemon's level is at least quadruple the target's
- Purpose: Rewards returning later with a stronger team and makes backtracking
  catches smoother without making them free

9) Lure Ball
- Role: Fishing specialist
- Effect: 4.0x on Pokemon encountered by fishing, 1.0x otherwise
- Purpose: Creates a clean encounter-method niche instead of relying on type-based
  bonuses

10) Heal Ball
- Role: Utility capture ball
- Effect: 1.5x catch rate and the caught Pokemon is fully healed on capture
- Purpose: Makes it useful immediately instead of just flavor; good for captures
  you plan to use right away

WHY THIS BALL SYSTEM WORKS
--------------------------
This setup keeps the system clean and focused. The player decision tree becomes:

- Is it turn 1? Use Quick Ball
- Is the fight dragging on? Use Timer Ball
- Have I already caught this species? Use Repeat Ball
- Am I much higher level? Use Level Ball
- Was it hooked by fishing? Use Lure Ball
- Do I want to use it immediately after capture? Use Heal Ball
- None of the above? Use Great Ball or Ultra Ball

This creates meaningful item identity without too many overlapping gimmicks.

RECOMMENDED SLOT REPLACEMENT MAP
--------------------------------
If replacing weaker or less useful original ball slots:

- Premier Ball slot -> Quick Ball
- Safari Ball slot -> Level Ball
- Dive Ball slot -> Lure Ball
- Nest Ball slot -> Heal Ball

Luxury Ball:
- No longer needed as a major ball identity in this plan
- Can remain low priority flavor, be removed from importance, or be used as a
  spare implementation slot if needed

EARLY GAME AVAILABILITY PLAN
----------------------------
The user wants Poke Balls, especially Great, Quick, Timer, and Heal Balls,
available very early game.

Recommended early-game philosophy:
- The player should begin interacting with the new catch system almost immediately
- Specialized balls should appear early enough to shape behavior, but in limited
  quantities so they do not break progression
- Ultra Balls should still arrive later as the dependable premium option

EARLY GAME SHOP / DISTRIBUTION RECOMMENDATION
---------------------------------------------

Very early game (first town / first mart upgrade):
- Poke Ball: standard stock, unlimited
- Great Ball: available very early, but more expensive than normal
- Quick Ball: available very early in small quantities or with limited stock
- Heal Ball: available very early, either in shop stock or from NPC gifts
- Timer Ball: available very early, but ideally slightly later than Quick Ball
  if you want a softer ramp

Suggested structure:
1. First accessible Mart:
   - Poke Ball
   - Great Ball
   - Heal Ball

2. Early route / early side reward / second Mart expansion:
   - Quick Ball
   - Timer Ball

3. Midgame:
   - Repeat Ball
   - Lure Ball
   - wider shop access to Quick / Timer / Heal

4. Later:
   - Ultra Ball becomes common and unlimited

WHY EARLY GREAT / QUICK / TIMER / HEAL WORKS
--------------------------------------------
Great Ball early:
- Makes the player feel stronger without waiting too long
- Helps avoid early catch frustration

Quick Ball early:
- Immediately teaches the player that some catches reward fast action

Timer Ball early:
- Encourages a second capture strategy from the start

Heal Ball early:
- Gives practical utility and feels distinct from pure catch-rate balls

This is good because the player learns early that carrying a mix of balls matters.

BALANCE WARNING FOR EARLY ACCESS
--------------------------------
If Great, Quick, Timer, and Heal Balls are all available very early, balance should
be controlled through one or more of these levers:

- Price
- Limited stock
- NPC gifts in small quantity
- Shop expansion by badge count
- Slightly rarer field pickups

Recommended approach:
- Great Ball: early unlimited or near-unlimited
- Heal Ball: early unlimited or near-unlimited
- Quick Ball: early but initially limited
- Timer Ball: early but initially limited
- Ultra Ball: noticeably later to preserve progression

PRICING DIRECTION
-----------------
Suggested pricing logic, not final mandatory values:

- Poke Ball: cheap baseline
- Great Ball: affordable early premium
- Heal Ball: around Great Ball price or slightly below
- Quick Ball: more expensive than Great Ball
- Timer Ball: more expensive than Great Ball
- Repeat Ball: mid-to-high price
- Level Ball: mid-to-high price
- Lure Ball: situational, moderate price
- Ultra Ball: high but dependable premium price

General principle:
Specialist balls should be stronger in the right situation, but not so cheap that
they completely replace Great Ball or Ultra Ball as defaults.

FINAL RECOMMENDATION
--------------------
Use this final capture ecosystem:

Core:
- Poke Ball
- Great Ball
- Ultra Ball
- Master Ball

Specialists:
- Quick Ball
- Timer Ball
- Repeat Ball
- Level Ball
- Lure Ball
- Heal Ball

Early game priority:
Make Poke Ball, Great Ball, Heal Ball, Quick Ball, and Timer Ball available very
early so the player starts engaging with the new capture system immediately.

Best early rollout:
- Early unlimited: Poke Ball, Great Ball, Heal Ball
- Early limited: Quick Ball, Timer Ball
- Midgame unlock: Repeat Ball, Level Ball, Lure Ball
- Later common stock: Ultra Ball
