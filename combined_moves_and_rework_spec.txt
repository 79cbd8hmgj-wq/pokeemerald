POKEMON MOVE REWORK — BUFF-FIRST IMPLEMENTATION SPEC
Gen 3 + Selected Gen 4/5 Type Expansion
Format Goal: Single-file implementation sheet focused on buffs, with only minor nerfs and distribution warnings.

================================================================
CORE PHILOSOPHY
================================================================

Primary goal:
- Raise weak, inconsistent, or niche moves into real usability

Secondary goal:
- Keep already-strong moves mostly intact
- Use distribution limits before meaningful nerfs
- Only apply small nerfs when a move is clearly too oppressive in testing

Balance rules:
1. Weak moves get direct numerical buffs
2. Dead utility moves get repurposed instead of ignored
3. Strong moves stay strong unless they dominate
4. Signature-scale moves should usually be controlled by rarity, not gutted
5. Mid-tier attacks matter more than adding extra nukes

================================================================
FINAL VERDICT
================================================================

This move pool should be improved mainly through buffs, not major nerfs.
Most of the unhealthy feeling in the current set comes from:
- weak move floors
- inconsistent multi-hit attacks
- dead utility slots
- narrow type support in legacy moves

The cleanest Emerald-style rebalance is:
- buff bad moves hard enough to matter
- preserve premium moves unless they clearly dominate
- control top-end power through who learns the move
- avoid overreacting with heavy nerfs too early
